Rules are extremely important in any sport. If you don't know the rules than you can't really play the sport.
Football field:
A football field is 120 yards long and 53 Â½ yards wide. Each endzone is 10 yards long and they are 100 yards apart from each other. The middle of the field is the 50 yard line.
Every line on the field represents 5 yards at a time. A small distance from the sidelines are the hash marks. The football is placed on or in betweem
them on each play.
The players:
Each team can have 11 players on the field at a time. Defensive players can place themselves anywhere they want on their side of the ball and they may
move freely without passing the line of scrimmage. The offensive players on the other hand have to follow some rules that define their positions. 7 players must be
lined up on the line of scrimmage prior to the snap. The other 4 must be at leats 1 yard behind the line. The players on the line must be set and not move prior to the snap
but the other 4 can move around in the back field prior to the snap. Only the 4 players in the back may catch or run the ball.
Game format:
A football game is divided into 4 quarters. Each quarter is 15 minutes long. After 2 quarters, there is a small break for the players called half-time.
Time is counted while the plays are running and sometimes in between the plays as well. The time can also stop if certains stuff happens.
A play:
The offense tries to move the ball down field on each play by either throwing the ball of running it. The offense gets 4 tries (called downs) to
get the ball 10 yards. If they get past 10 yards, then they get another 4 downs. That process repeats until they get to the end zone. If they don't
manage to get 10 yards, then after 4th down, the ball is turned over to the other team.
The offense can lose possesion of the ball by scoring, interception, not getting 10 yards, fumbling the ball and the other team recovers, punting or kicking the ball
or getting tackled in their end zone for a safety.
Penalties:
Penalties happen when one team breaks a rule. If the defense commits a penalty, than the offense gets to move foward a certain amount of yards for free depending on the
penalty. If the offense commits the penalty then they have to move back a ceartain amount of yards depending on the penalty. Some penalty examples are false start, offside,
pass interference, holding, face mask, intentional grounding, roughing the passer, etc.
