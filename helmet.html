The helmets are one of the most and if not, it's the most important piece of equipment in football. Helmets are specifically designed to minimize
the risk of a head injury during the sport, such as a concussion or bone fractures in the face, etc. Every peice of the helmet is useful and will give your head
maximum protection against other players.

There are multiple components that make up a helmet. For example, the helmet has an outer shell, it has padding on the inside, a helmet has a face mask and a chin
strap as well. The next bit will explain the function of each component.

The outer shell:
The outer shells main function is to defelct the impact on your head. It's made up of a material called polycarbonate alloy. It's a perfect material for the outer
shell because it's strong, flexible and durable.

Protecting the face:
On the front of every helmet is something called a face mask. It is designed to give as much protection as possible to the face bones and mouth. Your type of face
mask depends on the position you play. For example, a Quarterback or receiver would traditionally wear a face mask protect the nose and the jaw while giving them a
better field of view. On the other hand, a linemen would wear a face mask that protects the entire face.

Padding:
Padding is there to provide you with extra safety and some comfort. Padding helps absorb the force of the impact to your helmet. The pads are made up of a material
called thermoplastic polyurethane. It's known for its elasticity and resistance to dirt created by sweat and skin oils.

The chin strap:
The chin strap keeps the helmet firmly secured onto someones head. A properly fastened chin strap is tight enough to prevent the helmet from falling off while getting
hit. It makes the helmet 100 times safer.
